Message From Dean

On behalf of the Faculty of Engineering (FoE), I would like to welcome you at the Far-western University. The FoE has come a long way since the establishment of the University in 2010. Today, the Faculty is a vibrant entity where enrollment is growing, infrastructure (labs and equipment) is improving, and the programs are receiving national and international attention. This incredible growth is expected to continue in future too. It has been a leader in engineering education in the Far-western region of Nepal and aims to build educational programs that prepare its students to make a difference in the world by contributing towards societal development. Now, it has been offering Bachelor's degree in Civil Engineering and Computer Engineering in its School of Engineering at Mahendranagar.

shadow

We also plan to start a Bachelor of Architecture from the academic year 2024. With its aim to become a leading institution for higher education in the engineering field, the FoE has also started a master's degree in construction project management (MSc CPM) from 2023 and will start Structural Engineering from 2025 at its School of Engineering. Students from diverse geographical regions of the country are admitted to pursue engineering education at the FoE. Students get admission through competitive entrance examinations conducted by the FoE. Despite the lowest fee structure among the Engineering institutions in Nepal, we provide quality education and the best academic environment to the students with the support of its young, dynamic, and well-educated faculties.

To promote the sharing of knowledge, research, and innovative ideas, and enrich students' learning processes, we invite scholars and professionals from around the globe and encourage our students to participate in seminars and discussions. In addition, we have proposed to establish a Centre for Engineering Research and Innovation at our Central Campus to fulfill the need of carrying out research, development, and innovation. The FoE has also been attempting to tie up with international universities to promote mutual collaboration on research and education. We encourage our faculty members and students to conduct research and innovative work in the engineering field.

Indeed, success in the engineering field requires more than strong technical knowledge and the capacity to apply it to real-world problems. It also requires the ability to engage in close collaboration, as well as qualities like integrity, curiosity, creativity, open-mindedness, and a willingness to engage in innovation. I encourage the students to enhance their academic experience by being fully engaged with their professors, peers, and the staff who are here to support them throughout their educational journey. I welcome you to the Faculty of Engineering to pursue your degrees and actively participate in research and innovation activities.

Warmest Regards,
Assoc. Prof. Kishan Datta Bhatta, PhD.
Dean, Faculty of Engineering, Far-Western University
Mahendranagar, Kanchanpur, Nepal
